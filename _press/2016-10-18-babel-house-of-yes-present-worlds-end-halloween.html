This Halloween weekend, two of New York nightlife’s leading creative and party communities, BABËL and House of Yes, bring NYC an immersive theatrical Halloween party experience like no other on Friday, October 28 with World’s End Halloween. Welcoming those who dare to enter an unruly metropolis where society’s foundations and institutions have collapsed, guests will explore and discover secrets and surprises across five rooms at a to-be-revealed Secret Bunker location in Brooklyn. House of Yes will be showcasing the absolute finest in artwork, decor and the performing arts, alongside theatrical immersive experiences, while BABËL brings a stellar musical lineup led by Lee Burridge with top of the line production, making World’s End Halloween the ultimate end-of-the-world experience for Halloween partygoers.

Guests are encouraged to come prepared for the unexpected as they enter a world with endless possibilities filled with encounters and performances around every corner. While costumes are mandatory, a pop-up shop outside of the Secret Bunker hosted by House of Yes will cater for those in need of some last minute outfit items.

As the world ends and is engulfed by darkness, searing performances of fire will illuminate with the Cult of Flame as self-forsaken gods lessen themselves to the ranks of humanity. Guests can expect to conform to the new societal norms as the Parade of Flesh roams freely and accept assistance from bathroom goddesses that offer gifts of cleanliness to make the end of the world experience more palatable. For those needing to take a step back from the madness, they will find peace of mind in the Oasis Lounge or the more daring can dive deeper into the rabbit hole at the Secret Dungeon. As night turns to day, those who have emerged from the apocalypse will rise from the dust into a utopian afterlife with Naturalistic Rituals by the Hybrid Movement Company, whilst taking part in a celestial closing ceremony with House of Yes collaborator and vocalist Daisy Press, and finally indulge in a sunrise breakfast service with an abundance of fresh fruit and peace for the new world order.

With a reputation synonymous with elaborate parties and creative music, underground icon and All Day I Dream DJ Lee Burridge leads the night with musical support from rhythmic nomad Nu (live), Do Not Sit On The Furniture club owner and DJ Behrouz, deep and warm melodies from YokoO, an organic live set from Belgian four-piece Stavroz, plus deep house ace, Hoj.

Launched in 2013, BABËL is a collective of artists, musicians, and tastemakers that have formed a community upon beauty and authenticity. Serving as a platform to experience freedom and magic, past New York events have been held at Diamond Horseshoe, The Bowery Hotel, and Queen of the Night. Now in its third iteration, House of Yes is Bushwick’s newest unique and ultra-stimulating performance and party space that has changed the landscape of New York nightlife. As the second collaborative event between the two New York City creative powerhouses, World’s End Halloween offers partygoers an unmatched party experience where individual guest participation is encouraged and elevates the experience of all.

About House of Yes:
House of Yes opened its third iteration in Bushwick at the beginning of 2016 to become a beacon of NYC nightlife after shuttering its doors too soon in East Williamsburg three years ago. Original partners Anya Sapozhnikova and Kae Burke, teamed up with Ilan Telmont and Justin Ahiyon to build out the best venue ever with a little help from their friends, seeding a Kickstarter campaign for $92,340 (original goal: $60,000). The space includes an art-deco restaurant parlor, a spectacularly decorated warehouse-sized performance space, a courtyard with kitschy repurposed decor, and a clandestine mini-club. House of Yes is now the home of circus spectacles, immersive cinema, burlesque and cabaret shows, aerial extravaganzas, nightlife parties, morning raves, brunch, BBQs, and bubble baths, all with superior sound and exemplary service. Each night is specially curated to create an immersive experience with music, theatrics, and performances to titillate the senses and expand your mind, unlike any other venue in Brooklyn right now.
