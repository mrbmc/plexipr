HAUS OF YAS: DRAG LIFE DANCE PARTY
Music by Nomi Ruiz And Many More
Gender Bender Adventure With Assjazzling, Pop Up Strip Club, Glamour Shot Photobooth, Manicures & Pedicures And Much More
Friday, July 22 at House of Yes
Free before 11 pm with RSVP, $15.00 pre-sale tickets available here. $20 at the door.

House of Yes recently made their celebrated debut in the NYC Pride March, with a stunning float presenting a 60-foot tall, rainbow-draped mobile version of the venue’s nightlife shenanigans, overflowing with 35 resident aerialists and New York’s best nightlife entertainers, and a procession that included a pole (with pole dancers!) and full sound system — which all in all, “brought the party with their towering ship-like vehicle and legions of half-naked dancers,” according to Gothamist. To keep the party going, the Bushwick party palace is proud to present the first-ever Haus of Yas: Drag Life Dance Party on Friday, July 22.
The Drag Life Dance Party promises to be the most adventurous Friday night extravaganza in NYC as guests are encouraged to flip the script and dive deep into the gender blender of sexual disorientation. The festivities include a pop-up strip club with open pole, make-over station, glamour shot photo booth, assjazzling, manicures and pedicures,  glam drag, butch drag, daddy drag, and then some! Music will be provided by Nomi Ruiz, the NYC-based singer/songwriter who contributed to Hercules and Love Affair's debut album and now fronts electro-pop sensation Jessica 6. The event's musical lineup also includes Carlos Jadraque, Williams Francis, DIN//VOID, Gerry Visco, Derek Piotr, and Exit sense.
As always at House of Yes, present as your non-normative ecstatic sexpression of everything or anyone you ever wanted to be.

About House of Yes:
House of Yes opened its third iteration in Bushwick at the beginning of 2016 to become a beacon of NYC nightlife after shuttering its doors too soon in East Williamsburg three years ago. Original partners Anya Sapozhnikova and Kae Burke, teamed up with Ilan Telmont and Justin Ahiyon to build out the best venue ever with a little help from their friends, seeding a Kickstarter campaign for $92,340 (original goal: $60,000). The space includes an art-deco restaurant parlor, a spectacularly decorated warehouse-sized performance space, a courtyard with kitschy repurposed decor, and a clandestine mini-club. House of Yes is now the home of circus spectacles, immersive cinema, burlesque and cabaret shows, aerial extravaganzas, nightlife parties, morning raves, brunch, BBQs, and bubble baths, all with superior sound and exemplary service. Each night is specially curated to create an immersive experience with music, theatrics, and performances to titillate the senses and expand your mind, unlike any other venue in Brooklyn right now.
